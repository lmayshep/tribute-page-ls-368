The Brothers Grimm

Germany's earliest history, her mythology, her customs, her laws, her tales, her language, her songs, her proverbs, and their traditional forms, should be collected with the greatest care and fidelity.

Early Years

1785: Jacob Ludwig Carl Grimm is born on January 4th in Hanau, Germany.
1786: Wilhelm Carl Grimm is born on February 24th in Hanau, Germany.
1791: The Grimm family moves to Steinau, where their father, Philipp Wilhelm Grimm, works as a district magistrate.
1796: Their father dies of pneumonia, causing financial hardship for the family.

Education

1798: The brothers attend the Friedrichsgymnasium in Kassel.
1803: Jacob graduates from the Friedrichsgymnasium as the top student.
1804: Wilhelm graduates from the Friedrichsgymnasium as the top student.
1805: Both brothers attend the University of Marburg, where they develop an interest in German folklore and philology.
1806: Jacob works as a research assistant in Paris under the legal scholar Friedrich Carl von Savigny.

Careers and Publications

1808: Jacob becomes a librarian at the court of Jerome Bonaparte, King of Westphalia, in Kassel.
1812: The brothers publish the first volume of their collection "Children's and Household Tales" (Kinder- und Hausmärchen).
1814: The second volume of "Children's and Household Tales" is published.
1815: The Grimms publish "German Legends" (Deutsche Sagen), a two-volume collection of German legends.
1825: Wilhelm marries Henriette Dorothea (Dortchen) Wild, who contributed to their folktale collection.
1830: The brothers move to the University of Göttingen, where Jacob becomes a professor and Wilhelm a librarian.

Politics

1837: The Grimms, along with five other professors (the Göttingen Seven), protest against the abolition of the Hanoverian constitution by King Ernest Augustus. They lose their university positions as a result.
1840: The brothers are invited to the University of Berlin, where they work as professors and continue their academic research.

Later Years and Death

1848: Jacob is elected to the Frankfurt National Assembly during the Revolutions of 1848 in Germany.
1852: The Grimms begin work on the "German Dictionary" (Deutsches Wörterbuch), a comprehensive dictionary of the German language.
1859: Wilhelm dies on December 16th in Berlin.
1863: Jacob dies on September 20th in Berlin.
